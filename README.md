# Endometrial chronodisruption in RIF
Recurrent implantation failure (RIF) remains one of the most challenging conditions in reproductive medicine. In this study, we investigated transcriptomic alterations related to the endometrial circadian cycle during the window of implantation using a systems biology approach. Differential gene expression analysis was performed with the limma package in RStudio using the primary dataset (GSE111974), identifying 24 differentially expressed genes. Gene Set Enrichment Analysis (GSEA) revealed coordinated downregulation of pathways involved in metabolism, mitochondrial bioenergetics, and the sleep–wake cycle. To further explore gene interactions, we performed Weighted Gene Co-expression Network Analysis (WGCNA) and validated the findings using the independent dataset GSE92324. This approach identified PPARGC1A as a key dysregulated hub gene. Overall, our findings suggest that RIF is not driven by disruption of the core circadian clock itself but rather by peripheral circadian misalignment accompanied by impaired mitochondrial adaptation in the endometrium during the window of implantation. These results provide new insight into the molecular mechanisms underlying RIF and highlight potential targets for future research. 

General objetivo of this TMF:
To characterize transcriptomic alterations in the molecular circadian clock in endometrial tissue from patients with recurrent implantation failure by integrating bioinformatic workflows for differential expression and biological network analysis, with the aim of identifying potential biomarkers of biological desynchronization during the WOI
